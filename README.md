# SmartGrow

**Empowering the Next Generation of Precision Agriculture Innovators**

SmartGrow is an educational initiative that bridges the gap between modern smart farming technologies and the next generation of STEM talent. Through a home-based **Smart Growing System (SGS)** and a structured **Smart Growing Curriculum (SGC)**, middle school-aged youth gain hands-on experience with the real-world technologies driving the future of U.S. agriculture.

---

## Why SmartGrow?

U.S. agriculture faces a convergence of urgent challenges — climate change, labor shortages, and rising production costs. Precision agriculture (also known as digital or smart farming) offers powerful, data-driven solutions to these problems. Yet sustaining this innovation requires a pipeline of young people who are fluent in smart technologies.

SmartGrow addresses this need at the root: by inspiring curiosity and building skills in students before they enter the workforce.

---

## Project Objectives

| # | Objective |
|---|-----------|
| 1 | **Stimulate interest** in smart technologies among middle school youth |
| 2 | **Enhance Smart Technology Literacy (STL)** for agriculture and beyond |
| 3 | **Engage students** in design, execution, and evaluation using smart tools |
| 4 | **Foster STEM identity** through outreach and extension activities |

---

## Core Components

### Smart Growing System (SGS)
A home-based, hands-on growing system that puts real precision agriculture hardware in students' hands. Participants observe, interact with, and draw conclusions from live sensor data — mirroring professional agricultural workflows.

### Smart Growing Curriculum (SGC)
A structured curriculum built around the SGS that:
- Builds foundational knowledge of smart technologies within an agricultural context
- Develops transferable Smart Technology Literacy applicable across industries
- Integrates design thinking and scientific inquiry
- Connects learning to real-world AFRI priority areas in agriculture systems and technology

---

## Alignment with National Priorities

This project directly supports the **USDA AFRI priority area: Agriculture Systems and Technology**, responding to documented needs identified by the U.S. Government Accountability Office (GAO, 2024) and leading researchers in precision agriculture education (Erickson et al., 2018; Kagan et al., 2022).

---

## Target Audience

- Middle school-aged youth (ages ~11–14)
- Educators and 4-H/extension program leaders
- Researchers in agricultural education and STEM outreach
