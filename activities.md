# Learning Activities

The SmartGrow curriculum integrates hands-on technology experiences with foundational STEM concepts. All activities are designed for middle school-aged youth (approximately ages 11–14) and delivered through a home-based **Smart Growing System (SGS)**.

---

## Curriculum Overview

The **Smart Growing Curriculum (SGC)** is organized into three learning phases:

### Phase 1 — Foundations of Smart Technology
Students are introduced to the core concepts of precision agriculture and smart technologies.

- What is precision agriculture?
- How do sensors work?
- Introduction to data collection and interpretation
- Overview of the SGS hardware and software

---

### Phase 2 — Design & Execution
Students take an active role in running their Smart Growing System, applying the scientific method to real-world growing conditions.

- Setting up the SGS in a home environment
- Programming and configuring sensors
- Collecting and logging environmental data (soil moisture, temperature, light)
- Forming and testing hypotheses based on sensor readings
- Adjusting growing conditions based on data analysis

---

### Phase 3 — Evaluation & Extension
Students analyze results, reflect on their experience, and connect their learning to broader agricultural and STEM careers.

- Interpreting collected data and drawing conclusions
- Comparing results across different growing conditions
- Presenting findings to peers and community members
- Exploring career pathways in precision agriculture and STEM fields
- Participating in STEM identity-building outreach events

---

## Skills Students Develop

| Skill Area | Examples |
|------------|---------|
| **Smart Technology Literacy** | Reading sensor data, understanding IoT systems |
| **Scientific Inquiry** | Designing experiments, analyzing results |
| **Data Literacy** | Logging, visualizing, and interpreting agricultural data |
| **Systems Thinking** | Understanding how plant, environment, and technology interact |
| **STEM Identity** | Connecting personal experience to real careers |

---

## Curriculum Standards Alignment

The SGC is designed to align with:
- **Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS)**
- **Common Core Mathematics** (data analysis and measurement)
- **Computer Science for All** frameworks
